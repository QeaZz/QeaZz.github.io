Plant Drone

A project made by Jesper Jansson, Stefano Vanerio and Ziyi Zhao in the Mobile Computing course at Chalmers University of Technology

Background

The why and what of the project

Why:
This project aims to fulfill the need of information assistance in gardens for medium-large sized private gardens and gardens shops. It helps the user to understand the needs of their garden and learn from it. The application is supposed to be the bridge before hiring a gardener.

Target group: 
Private garden owners with medium-large sized gardens and garden shops.

What: 
The applications is virtual place, where the user can check on how her garden is doing. She can get notified if any of her plants are in need of care, both in basic ways and more advanced. From lack water to detection of rare deseases and parasite attacks. This is to attempt to cure the plants in an early stage and prevent it from becoming too late.

Competitor Analysis

A competitor analysis was made on 3 other plant recognition applications. We found out about certain functionalities and could deside on what was most desirable for our own application. We also confirmed that there was a gap in the market for our application and that our idea was unique and never done before.

The Design

Plant drone is an application that monitorize the users garden to help them taking care of their garden. It provides a virtual library where the user can go to get an update on how the plants in her garden are doing. 

Application Functionalities

  • Plant identification/recognition
  • Recognition of diseases and parasites
  • Self flying of the drone
  • Auto-piloted drone flying
  • Recognition of water condition liked to weather forecast
  • Plant status (general information, watering condition, soil, diseases, weeds)
  • Plant journal
  • Critical state check (dry plants, broken branches etc.)
  • Gardener view (overview view)
  • Map that marks things to do
  • Connection to sprinklers and IOT in general
  • Client view (main view)
  • Low flight mode (checking plant status)
  • High flight mode (Update mapping)

The Design Process

3 Ideas

In the early part of the design process we developed three different ideas that all had the same concept and share the same goal. The ​goal was to provide a tool that was supposed to give the user all the necessary information about how to best take care of their plants and flowers. They would also be provided with additional information about the plant in order to understand and learn about it. The idea was that it in the end would hopefully allow inexperienced people to feel more comfortable with bringing home a plant and take care of it. Studies have shown that taking care of a plant have positive mental health effects, meaning that if more people can get their own plants, we can increase the happiness among the population.

Drone AR-based Plant Recognizer

A basic application for indoor plants and flowers. That will help new plant owners to get to know theiir plants better and how to take care of them.

An application that turns your indoor flower to a virtual friend, who you need to take care of. The health of the virtual flower depends on how your real plant is doing. Meaning iit reminds you to take care of it. 

A flying drone that can watch over your out door garden and sends status updates to the application. It can remind you about chores and help you detect rare issues wiith your plants.

2 Ideas

We desided to move on with only two ideas. The idea we desided to drop was the Minimalistic Plant Recognizer as it was the least advanced and least creative one, out of the three.  We were also more interested in how the other two would turn out after some more work.

Final Idea

We came to the decision that even if the Prototype 2 was harder to implement and more time consuming, it would be worth it as is will become a cooler portfolio piece.

The Results

To start with, have we accomplished what we wanted to? I would say that we have, we have created an application that fulfills our initial goals of providing a tool that is supposed to give the user all the necessary information about how to best take care of their plants and flowers. And that they will also be provided with additional information about the plant in order to understand and learn about it. We have used design methods to find out what users wants as well as to come up with a design that is consistent.

Of course there are room for inprovement, especially in the design of the application itself. The final design of the app was made in a short amount of time and could be improved. Although, it serves its purpose and is not too complicated to interacted with. 

The final prototype is prototyped in Figma and would need to be reconstructed in order to put it into a real mobile application. We have done some research on how to integrate an API to our application and we found out that plant.id could work for us. But it would need to be worked on futher for a complete implementation to the future application.
